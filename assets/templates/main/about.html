Deep crack detection

Cracks in infrastructure present a critical concern for public safety, demanding timely detection to facilitate effective maintenance. This study introduces Deep Crack, a ground-breaking deep-learning approach designed for image-based crack prediction. Harnessing the capabilities of CNNs, this study proposes a novel MobileNet architecture as the foundation, incorporating transfer learning to enhance the model's proficiency in recognizing cracks . The proposed research methodology encompasses rigorous data pre-processing, where advanced image augmentation techniques are employed to address data scarcity and enhance the model's adaptability. The proposed model undergoes comprehensive training and validation on a diverse dataset, showcasing its effectiveness in discerning images with cracks from those without. To further optimize the classifier, the proposed model is fine-tune with a custom top layer that integrates global average pooling and dense layers.
